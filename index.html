02 · Solution

Cold storage, delivered cold.

Glacial Storage is unlimited archival capacity, delivered through Canada Post. Mail us a drive. We tape it. We mail it back. No network, no egress, no nonsense.

  • Unlimited capacity — bounded only by Canada Post's parcel volume.
  • Air-gapped by physics — there is no socket to attack.
  • Sovereign Canadian residency — data never leaves the country.
03 · How it works

Two envelopes. One country.

1. Ship

Customer mails a Snowflake (USB) loaded with data, or an empty one for a read.

→

2. Igloo

We receive, verify, and write to LTO-9 tape. Snowflake is reformatted and recycled.

→

3. Return

For reads, we ship a Snowflake back loaded with the requested data. Standard postage included.

Glacial SLA: 3–14 business days coast-to-coast. Faster in the Maritimes. Slower in winter, by design.

04 · Product

Snowflake → IceCube → Igloo.

Snowflake

A 20 TB ruggedized USB-C drive. Tamper-evident seal. AES-256 at rest. Floats.

IceCube

A field-deployable 1 PB chassis. 50 Snowflakes in a hardened case. Any resemblance to existing products, real or fictional, is purely coincidental and the subject of ongoing correspondence with counsel.

Igloo

Our regional facility. LTO-9 tape library, ambient-cooled, staffed by a small team and one (1) good dog.

05 · Geography is a moat

Why Canada.

  • Free cooling. The country is, on average, cold. Our PUE is a rounding error.
  • National logistics. Canada Post serves every postal code in the country, including the ones with no road.
  • Last-mile sled. For Nunavut and the territories, we contract Inuit-owned sled operators in winter, float plane in summer.
  • Sovereign by default. Data never crosses a border. PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, and federal procurement rules — handled.
06 · Positioning

Slow on purpose.

Speed is a liability for archival data. Anything reachable in milliseconds is reachable by an attacker in milliseconds. Our latency floor is our security floor.

  • Ransomware-immune. No remote write path exists. None can be added without a forklift.
  • Compliance-friendly. WORM by default. Chain of custody is a paper waybill.
  • Predictable. A two-week SLA is a two-week SLA. No noisy-neighbour tail latency.
  • Honest. If you need it now, you should not have archived it.

08 · Moat

Four things hyperscalers cannot copy.

No metered egress. By design.

Postage is the egress, and standard postage is bundled into the subscription. There is no per-GB line item on our invoice — and there is no plan to add one.

Sovereign Canadian residency.

Data is written, stored, and shipped within Canada. PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, federal procurement — defaults, not add-ons.

Air-gapped by physics.

There is no internet-facing endpoint to compromise. Tape is not addressable from a keyboard.

Lowest-carbon archival.

Ambient arctic cooling, tape at rest draws zero watts, last-mile sled runs on kibble. ESG report writes itself.

09 · Business model

One bill. One year. No surprises.

Customers pay a flat annual subscription per terabyte stored. Standard postage, redundancy, tape refresh, and a fair-use read allowance are bundled. There is no metered egress, no API call charge, no "request" SKU.

Pricing is in the data room.
